Over 35 years of legal experience: Judicial, Criminal, Civil, and Corporate

Judge Carolyn Ellsworth was appointed by Governor Brian Sandoval on October 17, 2011, from among the three finalists selected by the Nevada Commission on Judicial Selection to fill a vacancy in Department 5 of the Eighth Judicial District Court. She was elected to retain her seat in the November 2012 general election by an overwhelming margin of 75.45% of the vote.

Senator Richard Bryan speaking at Judge Ellsworth's Investiture

Prior to her appointment, Judge Ellsworth had practiced law in Clark County, Nevada, since 1980. She received her Juris Doctorate from Southwestern University School of Law in 1979 and joined the Clark County District Attorney’s Office in 1979, serving first as an appellate law clerk and then a Deputy District Attorney and Chief Deputy District Attorney.

During her 11 years in the District Attorney’s Office, she headed the Office's Consumer Fraud Unit and later the Major Fraud Unit. She also prosecuted hundreds of felony cases, including capital murder prosecutions; served as one of the two deputies designated to respond to suspected arsons; and wrote the office’s protocol for the handling of child sexual assault cases. During this same period Judge Ellsworth was appointed as a Special Assistant United States Attorney to assist the Department of Agriculture in the prosecution of fraud cases, and she taught a semester course in Criminal evidence at the Clark County Community College.

In 1990, Judge Ellsworth left her position with the District Attorney’s Office to assume the position of Assistant General Counsel to Mirage Resorts Incorporated, and Vice president and General Counsel to Mirage Resorts Risk Management. In those roles, she served as the chief in-house litigation counsel for Mirage Resorts and its many operating subsidiary companies, trying civil lawsuits in jury trials, handling workers compensation litigation, and supervising the risk management legal department.

After a decade in corporate practice, in 2000, Judge Ellsworth entered general private practice where she emphasized representation of employees in wrongful termination cases, including age, race and gender discrimination matters. Being a private pilot herself, she also developed a practice in aviation litigation representing both plaintiffs and defendants in cases involving aircraft accidents.

In 2007, Judge Ellsworth joined the Nevada Secretary of State’s Office, Securities Division, as its Chief of Enforcement. In 2009, she was appointed by Secretary of State Ross Miller as the Administrator of the Securities Division, enforcing Nevada’s securities laws, investigating complex criminal fraud cases for prosecution, supervising a staff of 20, and conducting investigations concerning alleged voter fraud.

Since her appointment as a District Court Judge, Judge Ellsworth has developed a reputation as being a hardworking, knowledgeable, fair and courteous judge who works tirelessly for the citizens of Clark County. She has guest lectured at various law firms and UNLV, and volunteered as a judge in Mock Trial presentations at Boyd Law School and the undergraduate program at UNLV. She has also been an active participant in the State Bar’s Transitioning Into Practice mentoring program.
