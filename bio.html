Judge Ellsworth has lived in Las Vegas since 1979. She has been married for 34 years to her husband, Craig Delk, and is the proud mother of three children: LT. Richard Delk, a Flight Officer in the United States Navy; Elisabeth Delk, a first-year law school student; and Jackson Delk, a public school high school sophomore.

Her professional and community activities have included serving as President of the Southern Nevada Association of Women Attorneys, Vice President of Nevada Women’s Lobby, President of W.E.C.A.N, Inc. (Working to Eliminate Child Abuse and Neglect) as well as memberships in the National District Attorneys’ Association, the Defense Research Institute, the National Employment Lawyers Association and the North American Securities Administrators Association.



*Unsung Champion Award* for work on behalf of Nevada's children

Carolyn has been a guest lecturer at UNLV and a judge in trial advocacy competitions in both the undergraduate and Law School programs at UNLV. She has also volunteered as a judge at the Adelson High School’s moot court program practice sessions. Judge Ellsworth also actively participates in the Nevada State Bar Association’s Transitioning Into Practice (TIP) program, which mentors new lawyers during their first year of practice, and in the State Bar's One Campaign, which encourages actively practicing lawyers to take pro bono cases.

Judge Ellsworth is a Master Gardener in the University of Nevada Cooperative Extension Master Gardener program, performing 50 hours of volunteer work per year teaching community members how to garden in the desert of Southern Nevada. She is also a private pilot (certified for fixed wing, single engine land, with a high performance aircraft rating), a certified SCUBA diver, and gourmet cook. She enjoys Western riding, and frequently rides her horse "Steel Gem" in local parades. In her spare time, Carolyn enjoys knitting, crocheting, alpine skiing, and marine aquarium management.
