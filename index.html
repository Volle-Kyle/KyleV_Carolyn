Since I was first appointed to the bench in 2011 by Governor Sandoval and later elected in 2012, I have worked hard to validate the trust placed in me as your District Court Judge in Department 5. I know our legal system works only when the system is fair and impartial to all, and the public places great faith in the judges they elect to ensure that the system operates without bias. I am honored by the trust placed in me, and believe that my record will show that I have upheld that trust.

Since the goal of a trial is the fair and just resolution of disputes, judges need a thorough understanding of the rules of evidence, keen analytical abilities, excellent oral and written skills, a calm and respectful manner towards others, the ability to empathize with the concerns and personal challenges of the participants, and a broad understanding of the law. These skills are only acquired through many years of experience as a practicing lawyer.

During my thirty-four years of legal practice, I have been fortunate to have practiced extensively in both the criminal and civil arenas, and in each of those areas I have represented parties on both sides – prosecution and defense, plaintiff and defendant. This experience gives me the broad background needed to handle the variety of cases brought in Clark County's District Courts.

When I took the bench in mid-October of 2011, Department 5 had a backlog of cases, and so my top priority at that time was to reduce the backlog through the hard work of moving cases expeditiously though the system. By the end of 2012 I had achieved that goal and returned Department 5’s docket to a working status compliant with the case management goals set by the Eighth Judicial District Court. The public rightly expects its judges to render just and reasoned decisions in every case and to save litigants needless time and expense occasioned by unnecessary delay. It remains one of my top priorities to continue meeting these important expectations.

Three decades of legal practice have taught me many things in addition to the law – among them patience, civility, empathy, and respect for others no matter their individual circumstances. I will continue to use all that I have learned should I again be honored with the public’s trust in being re-elected as a Judge on the District Court bench.

Thank you for taking the time to visit my website and learn more about my background and qualifications. I hope to retain your trust and once again earn your vote.





Carolyn Ellsworth and husband Craig Delk
